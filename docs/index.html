REFEDGE

A movie about a null result.

Scroll. Watch the ball. The study unfolds.

The question

Do referee crews leave money on the total?

Some crews call more fouls. Some slow the game. Assignments are public before tip. Maybe the market underreacts.

The setup

Three officials walk onto the floor.

We track rolling foul, FTA, pace, and tech rates — only from games they worked before tonight. No peeking.

The control

The market already has a number.

Closing total goes in as a feature. The model’s only job is incremental edge — not reinventing the whole score.

The test

Baseline vs treatment. Same folds.

Walk-forward by season. LightGBM twice: once without refs, once with. Then shuffle the crews and try again.

The shot

No edge.

Crew features made out-of-sample log-loss slightly worse. Permutation p ≈ 0.57. The “signal” is noise.

The caveat

One Polymarket season isn’t proof either.

Fractional Kelly on pre-tip prices lost money. Thin liquidity. The statistical null already said enough.
